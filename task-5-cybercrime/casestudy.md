\# Phishing Attack Case Study

Phishing is a cybercrime where attackers trick users into revealing sensitive information. In a typical phishing attack, a student receives an email that appears to be from a bank or payment service. The message asks the student to verify their account by clicking on a link. The link leads to a fake website that looks similar to the original one.

The student enters login credentials and OTP, which are captured by the attacker. Once the attacker gains access, they transfer money or misuse personal information. Students are often targeted because they frequently use UPI payments and online banking.

The consequences include financial loss, privacy breach, and stress for the victim. In some cases, recovering the money becomes difficult. This case highlights the importance of verifying links, not sharing OTPs, and using two-factor authentication. Awareness and careful online behavior can prevent phishing attacks.
